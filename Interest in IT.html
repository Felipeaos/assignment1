My Interest in Information Technology

It is very widely accepted that Information Technology (IT) is present in most situations in our contemporary lives.

Everywhere around us, an IT item is present. From mobile phones, Telecommunication towers, computers, GPS devices,
streaming services, smart televisions and home appliances, internet services, social media, distance education, etc.,
our modern lives are fully surrounded by technology.

My interest in IT started at a very young age playing video games, both on console and personal computers. During the
teenage years there was also a growing interest in online chats, peer to peer (P2P) software, web-based tools for school
research, travel planning, online banking, shopping, etc.
Recently the interest in IT has been strengthening towards business tools such as website building (utilising templates from
web hosts), integration with different payments and booking systems, cloud accounting, performance, task tracking, GPS
applications, etc.
In fact, year after year I have been spending more and more time working on a great number of systems which gives me a
high amount of satisfaction. The time increased and satisfaction generated by this has triggered a need to learn more about
Information Technology.

Running my service business and managing a small team whilst having a young family, an online course seemed like the best
solution in order to upgrade my skills, and the choice for RMIT was based on their reputation, experience in IT and flexibility.
My current interests are related to business software development and Artificial Intelligence. Further, I intend to seek work in
these fields however I am open to all the opportunities that may catch my interest along the course especially knowing that
Information Technology has a broad range of opportunities in areas that do not even exist at this moment.
